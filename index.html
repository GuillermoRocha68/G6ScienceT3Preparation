Activity 2: Reading

Energy All Around Us

Energy is all around us. It is the ability to do work or make things happen. There are many different types of energy in our world. Let's look at three very common types: sound energy, light energy, and heat energy.

First, what is sound energy? Sound energy comes from movements called vibrations. When an object moves back and forth very fast, it pushes the air and makes a sound. We use our ears to hear it. When you talk to a friend, listen to loud music, or play a wooden guitar, you are using sound energy. Even a dog barking is sound energy!

Second, there is light energy. Light energy travels in straight lines and helps us see the world. Without light, everything would be completely dark. The biggest and most important source of light energy is the sun. We also get light from electric lamps, candles, and flashlights. Green plants use light energy from the sun to grow and make their own food.

Third, we have heat energy. Heat energy is also called thermal energy. It makes things feel warm or hot. Like light, the sun gives us a lot of heat energy. A campfire, an oven, and a winter heater also make heat. We use heat energy every day to cook our delicious food, boil water, and keep our bodies warm when it is cold outside.
All these types of energy are very important. We use sound, light, and heat energy every day!

Glossary
  • Ability: The power or skill to do something.
  • Boil: To heat water until it gets very hot and makes bubbles.
  • Common: Happening often; seen in many places.
  • Delicious: Tasting very good.
  • Electric: Using electricity to work (like a lamp or a TV).
  • Movements: Changing places or positions.
  • Object: A thing that you can see and touch (but is not alive).
  • Source: The place where something comes from.
  • Thermal: Related to heat or temperature.
  • Vibrations: Fast movements back and forth.
